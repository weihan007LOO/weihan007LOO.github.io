Overview

GripSmart is an AI-powered home rehabilitation system designed to support hand recovery through intelligent monitoring and interactive therapy. Developed for the 2026 National Health Hackathon, the system combines a sensor-integrated smart gripper, mobile application, machine learning, and gamified rehabilitation to enable patients to perform guided exercises at home while allowing therapists to monitor rehabilitation progress remotely.

Awards & Honors

GripSmart was recognized as a Top Finalist and received the Team Spirit Award at the 2026 National Health Hackathon, organized by the Asia Innovative Healthcare Network, DGA Healthcare Group, MRANTI, and Cradle. The project was acknowledged for its innovative approach to digital rehabilitation, collaborative teamwork, and potential to improve accessibility to home-based recovery.

Problem

Hand rehabilitation often requires frequent hospital visits and continuous supervision by therapists, creating challenges such as:

  • Limited access to rehabilitation services for patients at home
  • Difficulty ensuring rehabilitation exercises are performed correctly
  • Lack of objective progress tracking between clinical visits
  • Reduced patient motivation during repetitive rehabilitation exercises
  • Limited personalization of recovery plans based on patient performance

These challenges can slow recovery and reduce the effectiveness of rehabilitation programs.

Solution

GripSmart integrates a sensor-equipped rehabilitation gripper with a mobile application, machine learning models, and interactive games to deliver personalized home-based therapy. The system measures grip strength and hand movement in real time, while embedded sensors verify that exercises are performed correctly. Machine learning models analyze rehabilitation performance to monitor recovery trends and support personalized training recommendations. Through Unity-based rehabilitation games, patients are encouraged to complete exercises in an engaging environment, improving motivation and adherence while allowing therapists to remotely monitor progress.

Key Features

  • Smart Rehabilitation Gripper

    Measures grip strength and hand exercise performance using integrated sensors

  • Correct Movement Detection

    Verifies exercise posture and movement to ensure proper rehabilitation

  • ML-Based Recovery Analysis

    Uses machine learning to monitor rehabilitation progress and predict recovery trends

  • Personalized Training Plans

    Recommends rehabilitation intensity based on patient performance

  • Gamified Rehabilitation

    Interactive Unity-based games make rehabilitation more engaging and motivating

  • Mobile App Integration

    Enables patients to track progress and perform guided rehabilitation at home

  • TinyML Edge Intelligence

    Supports lightweight on-device inference for efficient real-time analysis

Future Development

Future development will focus on collecting larger and more diverse rehabilitation datasets to improve the accuracy and generalizability of the machine learning models. Additional rehabilitation exercises and games can be introduced to support patients with different recovery stages and hand conditions. Integration with wearable devices and remote healthcare platforms will further enable therapists to monitor patient progress, adjust rehabilitation plans remotely, and provide more personalized care.
